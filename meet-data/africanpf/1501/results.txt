African Powerlifting Championships 2015
For the first time since the African Powerlifting Federation opened the African Championships to female
lifters, the South African Powerlifting Federation took a small team of 10 lifters to Morocco for the 2015
Championships, which took place from 23-25 October in the city of Meknes.

The South African team, consisting of Protea lifters and lifters competing under SAPF Federation colours,
was the only team from the southern hemisphere, with the other participants being Algeria, Egypt and
the host nation of Morocco. Three other countries were late cancellations due to logistical challenges.

The competition consisted of a Classic (Raw) Powerlifting event and a Classic Benchpress event, for age
divisions Junior, Senior, Master 1 and Master 2. Notwithstanding the small number of participants, the
South African team can be extremely proud of their results, achieving 10 gold medals and 1 silver medal.
Many personal bests were achieved, and Junior Protea lifter Megan Leighton set two new South African
records, with a benchpress of 62.5kg in the 57kg division.

Mens Junior 120kg                  Sebastiaan Loubser                 Gold


Congratulations also go to coach Ephraim Khandanise, who was awarded his Category 2 International
Referee certification at the competition.

The Best Team award went to Algeria, with their lifters Mohamed Bouafia and Safia Kouri taking the
Best Male and Female lifter awards.

At the APF Congress, bids were submitted by South Africa and Egypt to host the 2016 African
Powerlifting Championships. The bid was awarded to Egypt, subject to the APF executive approving the
suitability of the venue (Sharm El Sheikh) and the readiness of the EPF to host the event.
