African Powerlifting and Bench Press Championships 2016
The 2016 African Powerlifting and Bench Press Championships were held from the 19 th to the 22nd of
October in Potchefstroom, with participating teams from Algeria, Morocco, Libya, South Africa and guest
country Iran. Teams from Cameroon, Ghana and Egypt had to withdraw due to visa and/or financial
constraints.

There were 95 entrants in the 6 events, which were Men’s and Women’s Classic (Raw) Bench Press,
Equipped Bench Press and Classic (Raw) Powerlifting. The South African team consisted of 26 men and 22
women, made up of Protea and Federation lifters across the Junior, Open, Master 1 and Master 2 age
categories.

APF news
At the African Powerlifting Federation Congress meeting held on 19 October, outgoing APF president Alan
Ferguson handed over the presidency to Moussa Messaour from Algeria. Alan will complete his term (of
which there are three years remaining) on the IPF executive as EC representative from Africa. The new
APF board is as follows:

President               : Moussa Messaour (Algeria)
Vice President          : Heather Leighton (South Africa)
General Secretary       : Elmamoun Mohammed (Morocco)
Treasurer               : Hamid Jamal (Morocco)
Technical Chairman      : Hannie Smith (South Africa)
IPF EC representative   : Alan Ferguson (South Africa)

It was agreed to set standards for African Powerlifting records, which will be done by Hannie Smith and
Heather Leighton, as a percentage of the existing World records. The APF will follow the Commonwealth
rules regarding claiming records (Can be done at an International comp only, must have 3 refs from
different countries and a jury of 3 refs from different countries, must have drug testing at the comp).
Heather Leighton will be Records Registrar.

The next African Powerlifting Championships will be hosted as follows:

2017 – Algeria
2018 - Morocco
2019 – South Africa (possibly to be combined with the IPF World Masters Championships)
2020 - Libya
